Historical Places in India

The Taj Mahal is an ivory-white marble mausoleum in the Indian city of Agra.

The Qutub Minar is a minaret and victory tower located in Delhi.

Charminar is inspired from the Shia “tazias”.

The Temple is dedicated to Venkateswara, a form of Vishnu, of Kali Yuga.

The Elephanta Caves are located in Western India on Elephanta Island (otherwise known as the Island of Gharapuri), which features two hillocks separated by a narrow valley.

The Golden Temple is an open house of worship for all people, from all walks of life and faiths.

India Gate, also called Delhi Memorial, is a war memorial and the epitome of an architectural marvel in India.

The Lotus Temple, located in Delhi, India, is a Bahá'í House of Worship that was dedicated in December 1986.

Red Fort, also called Lal Qalʿah, also spelled Lal Kila or Lal Qila, Mughal fort in Old Delhi, India.

The "Great Stupa" at Sanchi is the oldest structure and was originally commissioned by the emperor Ashoka the Great of the Maurya Empire in the 3rd century BCE.
